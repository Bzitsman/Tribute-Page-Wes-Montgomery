John Leslie "Wes" Montgomery

One of the greatest guitarists in the history of jazz!

The life of Wes Montgomery:

  • March 6, 1923 Born in Indianapolis, Indiana.
  • 1935 His older brother Monk, bought him a tenor guitar from a pawn shop.
  • 1943 Worked as a welder and bought his first six-string guitar
  • 1948Was hired by Lionel Hampton and spent the two years in the band
  • 1957 Wes and his brothers move to the west coast and he signed his first record contract that year

Awards and honors:

  • 1960 Second Place, Readers' Poll, Metronome
  • 1690 Most Promising Jazz Instrumentalist, Billboard
  • 1960 Talent Deserving Wider Recognition, DownBeat
  • 1961 Readers' Poll and Critics' Poll, DownBeat
  • 1962 Readers' Poll and Critics' Poll, DownBeat
  • 1960-63, 1966, 1967 Best jazz guitarist, DownBeat magazine Critics' Poll
  • 1965 Two Grammy Award nominations
  • 1966 Grammy Award, Best Instrumental Jazz Performance by Large Group or Soloist with Large Group
  • 1966 Jazz Man of the Year, Record World
  • 1968 Grammy Award, "Eleanor Rigby" and "Down Here on the Ground"
  • 1968 Montgomery died of a heart attack on June 15, 1968, while at home in Indianapolis
  • 1969 Grammy Award nomination, Willow Weep for Me

Jazz guitarist Bobby Broom said that on A Dynamic New Sound in 1959, Montgomery "introduced a brand new approach to playing the guitar... The octave technique... and his chord melody and chord soloing playing still is today unmatched"

-- Bobby Broom on Wes Montgomery's 1959 Jazz Guitar Impact
